Sports PE and Commercial Rights Landscape
Two functions dominate European sports commercial infrastructure: brokers who sell rights they do not own, and acquisition firms that take equity stakes and compound returns through commercial centralisation. The two categories are converging. This maps both. As of April 2026.

Section 1 — Sponsorship Brokers
TEAM Marketing
Private — wholly owned by UEFA/UC3 joint venture
Mandate Ending 2027
Key Deals
  • 2024-27 UCL sponsorship cycle: nine global partners including Qatar Airways, Mastercard, Heineken, PlayStation, Crypto.com, Bet365, FedEx, PepsiCo/Lay's, JustEat. Reported $3.5bn total rights value across media and sponsorship.
  • Managed UCL, Europa League, and Conference League commercial rights for 35 years. That mandate ends 2027-28 after Relevent won the open tender process in early 2025.
Signal: TEAM retains account management for current 2024-27 cycle partners during transition, but the mandate does not renew. Thirty-five years of institutional knowledge exits the market.
Relevent Sports Group
Private — owned by RSE Ventures / Stephen Ross (Miami Dolphins owner)
UCL Mandate 2027-33
Key Deals
  • La Liga North America JV (2018): 15-year partnership; brokered La Liga's ESPN deal in 2021, the largest Spanish football media rights deal ever in North America.
  • UCL/Europa/Conference League US rights 2024-27: sold to CBS Sports, building the commercial relationship with UEFA that underpinned the 2027-33 bid.
  • UCL global mandate 2027-33: UC3 (UEFA/ECA JV) appointed Relevent as sole global commercial partner, replacing TEAM Marketing. Covers CL, Europa, Conference League, Super Cup, Youth League, and Futsal CL across all territories.
Signal: A US-owned commercial agency now controls the most valuable club competition rights package in world football. How Relevent structures and monetises the mandate across its first cycle will define the 2033 tender.
SPORTFIVE
Private — owned by Bertelsmann via Fremantle/RTL Group; 1,200 staff, 15 countries
German Dominant
Key Deals
  • Signal Iduna Park naming rights (Borussia Dortmund): brokered original deal and managed renewals. Germany's highest-profile stadium naming rights contract.
  • Commerzbank Arena naming rights (Eintracht Frankfurt): brokered and managed across multiple contract cycles.
  • FC Bayern Munich international partnerships: manages Bayern's regional sponsor relationships across Asia, MENA, and the Americas on a multi-year representation basis.
Model: Long-term rights representation contracts, typically multi-year exclusives. Commission on deals closed plus retainers on representation mandates. Expanding internationally via club representation.
Infront Sports & Media
Private — within Wanda Sports Group (HKG: 1691); Zug-based
France + Germany Exposure
Key Deals
  • Ligue 1 and Ligue 2 international media rights: exclusive mandate via LFP Media across 100+ territories, including technology and betting rights.
  • Bundesliga International (2024): distributes rights across nine Eastern European markets from 2025-26.
  • Icon League joint mandate with 360Media: international sponsorship and media rights for the influencer football competition.
Model note: Infront bundles media distribution with sponsorship sales in a single mandate — more control than a pure broker, without taking equity. Blurs the broker/distributor distinction.
IMG
Private — owned by Endeavor Group, which Silver Lake took private at $25bn, March 2025
Structural Conflict
  • The FA: long-term commercial and media rights adviser; manages international distribution and sponsorship packaging for English football's governing body.
  • Premier League international rights: distributor and commercial adviser across multiple overseas markets.
  • UCL 2027-33 mandate: bid in the open tender; Relevent was selected after exclusive negotiation period from February 2025.
Conflict: Silver Lake's Endeavor acquisition creates a structural conflict. IMG advises rights holders on deals while Silver Lake (via CFG stake) owns clubs whose revenues are directly affected by those same deals. Regulatory attention on this structure is likely.
Octagon
Private subsidiary of IPG (NYSE: IPG); $3.5bn+ in annual client sponsorship spend managed
Brand-Side Primary
  • Standard Chartered / Liverpool FC: managed the shirt sponsorship and developed the "Play On" women's football campaign.
  • Snickers / UEFA tournaments: multi-cycle strategy across European tournaments including the "Own Goal" campaign.
  • Movie & Media Sports acquisition: added European football and rugby governing body rights sales to its buy-side model.
Model distinction: Octagon is primarily brand-side — earns fees from corporates for sponsorship strategy, not commissions from rights holders. It sits on the opposite side of the value chain from SPORTFIVE and Relevent.
Section 2 — PE Acquisition Firms
CVC Capital Partners / Global Sport Group
Private — ~$180bn AUM; GSG formed 2025 to consolidate all sports holdings
Valuation Haircut
Key Deals
  • La Liga: $2.1bn for 10.95% of LaLiga's commercial rights vehicle over a 50-year term. 38 of 42 clubs voted in favour; Barcelona and Real Madrid opted out. Approximately half of GSG's earnings come from La Liga.
  • Ligue 1 / LFP Media: ~$1.5bn into the French league's commercial vehicle. Underperformed post-investment relative to La Liga, contributing to the GSG stake sale failure.
  • Premiership Rugby: $294m for 27%. Three of 13 clubs in the 2022-23 season subsequently went insolvent (Worcester, Wasps, London Irish).
Risk flag: CVC failed to sell an external GSG stake at a €9bn target. Instead closed a €3.5bn debt deal in March 2026 (KKR/Global Atlantic ~€1.4bn preferred equity; Pimco ~€1.5bn debt). Implied equity value ~€7-8bn versus $14bn internal target. Equity investors passed at CVC's asking price.
Silver Lake
Private — ~$100bn+ AUM; technology and media focus
Most Integrated
  • City Football Group (CFG): $500m for just over 10% in 2019, valuing CFG at $4.8bn. CFG owns Manchester City, Girona, NY City FC, and eight other clubs.
  • Endeavor take-private: $25bn deal, March 2025. Gives Silver Lake control of IMG, UFC, WWE, and On Location events business.
Conflict: Silver Lake simultaneously owns a Premier League club (Man City via CFG) and controls IMG, which advises on Premier League and UEFA commercial rights. Vertical integration of this kind creates systemic conflicts that regulators will eventually address.
Arctos Partners
KKR agreed to acquire Arctos at $1.4bn, early 2026; expected close Q2 2026. Fund II at $4.1bn AUM.
KKR Acquisition Pending
  • Paris Saint-Germain: minority stake via QSI, December 2023. Capital directed at stadium development and Poissy training centre expansion.
  • Fenway Sports Group: minority stake in FSG, owner of Liverpool FC. Liverpool generates over $900m in annual revenue.
  • Atalanta: minority stake; entered during peak commercial leverage (2024 Champions League finalists).
  • Aston Martin Aramco F1: sub-10% minority at £1bn team valuation, 2023.
Model: Non-controlling positions in clubs and parent holding companies. European exposure via PSG, FSG/Liverpool, Atalanta. KKR acquisition brings sports PE into one of the largest alternative asset managers globally.
Pylon Angle The Relevent/UCL mandate handover in 2027 is the structural shift to watch. A US commercial agency controlling the most valuable club competition rights in world football is a category-level change. CVC's GSG valuation haircut (from $14bn to ~€7-8bn implied) is the clearest public data point on what institutional investors think European sports rights are actually worth at scale. The gap between CVC's asking price and what buyers would pay signals that the first wave of sports rights securitisation has reached its ceiling. Silver Lake's dual position (CFG ownership + IMG control) is the conflicts question that regulators have not yet addressed but will.
