Live Opportunity
Alpine stake — Otro Capital exit: The one live transaction in F1 is Otro Capital's reported exploration of its 24% Alpine stake sale (Oct 2025). At the €200m entry in 2023 (implied $900m valuation), buyers entering now are paying a premium over that basis. Christian Horner's reported interest adds a credibility signal; no confirmed deal as of April 2026. This is the only accessible F1 entry point where price discovery is still open.
Pylon Angle Williams was acquired for ~$200m in 2020 and is now valued north of $2.5bn. McLaren transacted at $4.1bn in September 2025 versus a £1.2bn implied valuation at MSP's 2020 entry. The F1 repricing cycle is largely complete for established teams. The remaining opportunity set: Alpine's contested 24% stake, and structural access via fund vehicles (Arctos/KKR post-close). Direct control acquisition of a competitive team is not available at rational multiples. The Dorilton/Williams entry in 2020 was the last clean control deal in the cycle.
