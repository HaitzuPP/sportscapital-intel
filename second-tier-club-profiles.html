1. Deportivo La Coruna

La Liga 2  /  Spain

Ownership

Abanca, the Galician retail bank, holds approximately 80% of RC Deportivo de La Coruna. Abanca arrived involuntarily — a creditor during the club's 2013-14 insolvency that converted debt to equity when the prior regime collapsed. The controlling principal behind Abanca is Juan Carlos Escotet, the Venezuelan-born Spanish banker whose personal fortune is estimated at approximately $7.4 billion, Spain's fourth-largest. Escotet was ratified as club president in October 2024, shifting the posture from passive creditor to active principal. This is patient, regionally-anchored capital — there is no exit imperative and no pressure to generate short-term returns, which sets the pace of decisions accordingly.

Escotet is building beyond a single asset. Portuguese club Penafiel has joined the Abanca portfolio, signalling Iberian multi-club ambition. Any commercial conversation that references portfolio-level relevance or crossover with the Portuguese market will reach a broader decision set than a Deportivo-only pitch.

Day-to-day management sits with Massimo Benassi (CEO, appointed June 2024). Benassi is Italian, brought in from UD Ibiza and previously CD Leganes. Commercial and business development is handled by Santiago Vázquez Cobián, 8+ years at the club, providing continuity across ownership transitions.

The insolvency that placed Abanca in control was precipitated by former president Augusto Cesar Lendoiro, who ran the club from 1988 to 2013. Lendoiro built genuine European ambition, funding a squad that won La Liga in 1999-2000 and reached the Champions League semi-finals in 2004. The squad was assembled on credit: by the time Lendoiro stepped down in 2013, accumulated debts reached approximately EUR 160 million, at the time the largest insolvency in Spanish football history. The bank converted the debt and has held control since. Deportivo was relegated from the top flight in 2011, entered administration in 2012, fell further to the third tier by 2020, and spent four years in Spain's Primera Federacion before returning to professional football in 2024.

The stadium, Estadio Abanca-Riazor (capacity 34,611), is municipally owned by the City of A Coruna and operated by the club under a long-term lease. In 2024 the club and city agreed a 25-year lease extension. The Abanca naming rights were first agreed in 2017 for eight years and are being extended as part of the same package. The naming arrangement is the single largest commercial agreement in the club's current commercial portfolio.

Commercial Activity

Deportivo's commercial profile is modest, reflecting six years outside professional football between 2020 and 2024. Primary shirt sponsor for 2024-25 is Hijos de Rivera, the A Coruna brewery behind the Estrella Galicia brand. Kit manufacturing is handled by Kappa from 2024-25. No digital fan engagement product, fan token, or app has been confirmed. The club has leaned into Galician cultural identity as a commercial differentiator: the 2024-25 home shirt was a tribute to club legend Arsenio Iglesias, and a 2025 kit was produced to mark a club anniversary.

The most distinctive commercial asset is the Galician diaspora, concentrated in Argentina, Brazil, and Germany, with secondary communities across northern Europe. The diaspora creates a globally dispersed but emotionally attached fanbase that is difficult to monetise through traditional matchday revenue but well suited to digital engagement products. The club has named diaspora outreach as a marketing priority, though execution remains limited relative to the opportunity. Average attendance since returning to La Liga 2 in 2024-25 has settled above 21,000, among the stronger draws in the division for a club at this size and league level.

Deportivo spent four consecutive seasons in Spain's third tier before the 2023-24 promotion, a sobering stretch for a club that won La Liga in 2000 and reached the Champions League semi-finals in 2004 under Javier Irureta. Charlie Patino, the Spanish-born Arsenal academy product, arrived on a four-year contract in August 2024. Manager Oscar Gilsanz stabilised the side after Idiakez's dismissal in October 2024 and delivered a second-place finish in 2024-25, though Elche pipped Deportivo to automatic promotion on the final day. The club entered the playoffs, where it was eliminated. Squad depth remains limited relative to better-funded Segunda rivals.

Key note: The bank majority ownership is commercially significant in both directions. Abanca has no strategic interest in football and a natural incentive to see the club generate revenue without requiring capital injections. This creates openness to commercial partnerships at board level. The constraint is governance pace: decisions flow through an Abanca-aligned board structure rather than a commercially motivated owner with a personal investment thesis. Internal bandwidth is also constrained during an active promotion campaign.

2. UD Almeria

La Liga 2  /  Spain

Ownership

UD Almeria is majority-owned by SMC Group, a Saudi Arabian media, advertising, and technology conglomerate led by Mohamed Al-Khereiji. SMC completed a full ownership transfer from the club's previous owner, Turki Al-Sheikh, on 16 May 2025. Al-Khereiji assumed the position of Chairman of the Board of Directors on completion. In February 2026, Cristiano Ronaldo acquired a 25% stake through his CR7 Sports Investments vehicle, joining the SMC-led consortium as a minority shareholder. Financial terms were not disclosed.

Al-Khereiji's commercial background is directly relevant to understanding the club's ownership approach. He chairs Al Arabia Out of Home, one of the largest outdoor advertising networks in the Middle East, alongside hospitality assets including Elegant Hotels and restaurant brands including Benihana and Scalini. SMC describes itself as operating across media, advertising, and technology in the region. The group's media-first philosophy is relevant context for any commercial partnership conversation: an ownership group that runs advertising networks as its core business evaluates sponsorship proposals through a commercial lens rather than a sports-fan one. Ronaldo's involvement is publicly framed as a long-term strategic investment with commercial intent: "I wish to work with the team leading the club to support it in its new phase of growth," he stated on announcement. No debt overhang has been reported under either the Al-Sheikh or SMC ownership periods.

Turki Al-Sheikh had acquired Almeria in August 2019 for approximately EUR 20 million, funded the club's promotion to La Liga in 2022, and sold to SMC in May 2025. The transition was orderly and without public dispute over terms.

Commercial Activity

Kit manufacturing for 2025-26 is handled by Macron. The stadium, Power Horse Stadium (formerly Estadio de los Juegos del Mediterraneo), carries an active naming rights deal with energy drink brand Power Horse, signalling commercial openness to such arrangements. Specific front-of-shirt sponsor details for 2025-26 have not been publicly confirmed. The Ronaldo 25% stake acquired in February 2026 has immediately elevated the club's international media profile: his public message to the squad after a March 2026 win generated international press coverage that no La Liga 2 club would ordinarily attract. No fan token or Socios-style digital partnership has been confirmed, making the commercial slate clean.

Almeria's average attendance has historically sat below the top La Liga 2 clubs, reflecting the city's relatively small population base (approximately 200,000). The promotional context in 2025-26, combined with the Ronaldo involvement, has lifted domestic and international fan engagement. The club's commercial team has scope to leverage the Ronaldo brand equity well beyond its geographic market.

Almeria's trajectory is that of a well-funded but operationally inconsistent club. The 2021-22 Segunda title was dominant, but the club failed to establish itself in La Liga: narrow survival in 2022-23, then a 28-game winless streak through 2023-24 and last-place relegation with 21 points. Under SMC ownership and with Ronaldo's stake from February 2026, there is visible commercial and sporting momentum. Manager Rubi oversaw the 2021-22 promotion and has returned for the current campaign. Squad standout is Sergio Arribas with 13 goals in 2025-26. Promotion to La Liga in 2025-26 would materially change the club's broadcast revenue and commercial scale.

Ronaldo Nazario (Brazilian, two-time Ballon d'Or winner) held the majority stake at Valladolid and sold in May 2025. Cristiano Ronaldo (Portuguese) acquired 25% of Almeria in February 2026.

3. SSC Bari

Serie B  /  Italy

Ownership

SSC Bari is owned by FilmAuro, the film production and distribution company controlled by the De Laurentiis family. Aurelio De Laurentiis, who also owns and presides over SSC Napoli in Serie A, acquired the rights to form a phoenix club in 2018 following the bankruptcy and liquidation of the previous entity, AS Bari. The reformed club started in Serie D in 2018-19. Aurelio's eldest son, Luigi De Laurentiis, was appointed club president in August 2018 and has run Bari's day-to-day operations since, with the stated ambition of delivering promotion to Serie A.

The defining structural issue is the Italian football federation's ruling on multi-club ownership. FIGC has confirmed through its president Gabriele Gravina that the De Laurentiis family must divest one of the two clubs by July 1, 2028. Crucially, if Bari earns promotion to Serie A before that date, the forced divestiture would be triggered immediately, as no individual or entity can own clubs competing in the same Italian division simultaneously. Luigi De Laurentiis confirmed in December 2025 that the family is in active negotiations with two foreign companies interested in acquiring Bari. Earlier reports pointed to an unnamed American investment fund as the leading candidate. The De Laurentiis family has indicated a preference to remain as minority investors post-sale rather than exit completely, though the FIGC hard deadline limits their leverage in negotiations.

Commercial Activity

Bari's most significant physical commercial asset is the stadium. Stadio San Nicola, designed by Renzo Piano and constructed for the 1990 World Cup, holds 58,270, making it the third-largest club stadium in Italy. Average attendance in 2024-25 was approximately 15,900, meaning the ground operates at roughly 27% of its theoretical capacity. A promotion campaign would substantially lift that utilisation, with San Nicola capable of generating matchday revenues that would rank among the highest in the Italian second division. No naming rights deal exists; the stadium retains its Stadio San Nicola designation, representing an open commercial opportunity.

Partial renovations completed in late 2023 included new petal coverings and lighting at an approximate cost of EUR 9.9 million. Main shirt sponsor for 2024-25 is Granoro, an Apulian pasta manufacturer with a multi-season relationship with the club. Kit manufacturing is handled by Erreà. The Casillo family (Puglia-based grain and milling) holds shorts sponsorship. Commercial revenues are regionally concentrated; there is no evidence of national or international commercial deal-making consistent with the club's promotion ambitions or the scale of its stadium infrastructure.

The adjacent context at the parent club is relevant. SSC Napoli has an active and expanding Socios fan token partnership. In December 2024, Napoli and Socios.com expanded their partnership with enhanced benefits including binding club polls, signed merchandise, and match ticket access for $NAP holders. The token trades across 12 active markets with a circulating supply of approximately 4.2 million tokens, available in 103 countries. The De Laurentiis family's direct operational familiarity with the fan token model at Napoli is material context for any commercial conversation at Bari.

Serie D to Serie B in four seasons is a credible execution of the phoenix club model, built through patience, professional management, and modest but consistent capital deployment. The ceiling is now visible: three Serie B seasons without a realistic promotion challenge, with the 2028 divestiture deadline constraining the capital investment that closing the gap to Serie A would require. Moreno Longo has maintained stability without generating upward momentum. Without new ownership capital, the club is likely to remain a solid Serie B side rather than a Serie A candidate.

Key note: The 2028 FIGC deadline creates a defined sale timeline and a structural incentive to build commercial value ahead of a transaction. Any buyer conducting due diligence on Bari will assess the commercial infrastructure in place at the time of sale. The stadium operating at 27% capacity is simultaneously a current revenue constraint and a clear upside argument for any incoming owner willing to invest in promotion and fan engagement.

4. Sporting de Gijon

La Liga 2  /  Spain

Ownership

Orlegi Sports, the Mexican multi-club group led by Alejandro Irarragorri Gutierrez, acquired 73% of Real Sporting de Gijon on 28 June 2022 for a reported EUR 43 million, buying out the Fernandez family who had controlled the club for three decades. Irarragorri became the first foreign president in the club's history. Orlegi's broader holding, Grupo Orlegi, operates across sports, real estate, and media in Mexico. Atlas FC, previously Orlegi's headline Liga MX asset, was sold to Grupo Prodi in April 2026 for approximately $250 million. Following that sale, Gijon carries greater strategic weight within Orlegi as its principal European asset alongside Club Santos Laguna.

José Riestra López became Executive President of Sporting de Gijon in December 2025, replacing David Guerra. He is the day-to-day decision-maker at club level. Commercial and B2B partnerships are handled by Jorge Morales Concepción (Director Comercial B2B), who has a background in Spanish sports radio across SER, Onda Cero, and COPE. Both are LinkedIn-confirmed. Any commercial approach should route through Morales for initial contact, with Riestra as the escalation path.

The financial position is the primary risk indicator. Auditors raised concerns about the club's financial viability, with total debt reaching EUR 42.3 million, of which approximately EUR 24 million relates to the CVC Impulse Plan. The CVC debt position and any associated covenants are relevant to how commercial deal revenue can be structured and distributed. Orlegi has demonstrated commitment to a long-horizon infrastructure play at Gijon, but the auditor concerns and debt load create pressure for commercial revenue generation.

Commercial Activity

Gijon plays at El Molinon, the oldest professional football ground in Spain, with a capacity of approximately 30,000. Average seasonal attendance exceeds 400,000 per year, roughly 21,000 per match, representing genuine fan density for a La Liga 2 club. In February 2024, Orlegi unveiled a EUR 150 million stadium renovation plan designed by Mexican architecture studio Sordo Madaleno, targeting completion by 2029 and incorporating 12,600 square metres of commercial space. Total revenue grew 24% year-on-year in 2023-24, indicating that Orlegi is successfully building the commercial base even if absolute levels remain modest.

The club's Instagram following stands at approximately 196,000, notably low relative to its physical attendance base of 21,000 per match, indicating that digital engagement is underdeveloped as a commercial channel relative to the live fanbase. No Socios or fan token arrangement has been established. Orlegi's Liga MX clubs have historically explored digital fan engagement products, providing some ownership-level familiarity with the category even if no Gijon-specific deal has been executed.

5. CD Leganes

La Liga 2  /  Spain
Ownership confirmed active: Blue Crow Sports Group remains the owner of CD Leganes. The Le Havre AC acquisition in summer 2025 while Blue Crow still held Leganes confirms continued ownership and expanding multi-club commitment. The group-level commercial function is operated by Iván Maroto (VP Business and Global Partnerships, Blue Crow group level), whose mandate covers the full portfolio — Leganes, Le Havre, and Cancun FC. Pitches framed at group level rather than Leganes-only will reach a broader mandate and get more traction. Maroto is LinkedIn-confirmed and is the primary commercial entry point.

Ownership

Blue Crow Sports Group acquired 99.1% of CD Leganes in June 2022 for a reported $39 million. Blue Crow was founded by Jeff Luhnow, former General Manager of the Houston Astros (dismissed in January 2020 following the Major League Baseball sign-stealing investigation). Luhnow has built Blue Crow around data-driven sports management, bringing Sarah Rudd, an early pioneer of modern football analytics and former Arsenal employee, as VP of Analytics. The group has stated an ambition to own eight clubs by 2030.

885 Capital, the Dubai-based investment firm co-founded by Sudeep Ramnani and Jai Mahtani, subsequently acquired a significant stake in Blue Crow, placing both founders on the board. 885 Capital operates across PFL MMA and the Baller League alongside its Blue Crow position. 885 Capital previously backed Paystack, acquired by Stripe for $200 million in 2020. The UAE-anchored capital base and the data-driven management philosophy represent a materially different ownership profile from family-owned Spanish football clubs. Blue Crow's portfolio also includes Le Havre AC, acquired in summer 2025 while the club was in Ligue 1, and Cancun FC in Mexico's second division.

Commercial Activity

Leganes plays at Estadio Ontime Butarque with a capacity of 14,500. Logistics company Ontime holds stadium naming rights through 2029. Baghdadi Capital, a family office services group, holds a four-season premium shirt sponsorship. Ebury, the international payments fintech, is a commercial partner, a connection consistent with the fintech-adjacent investor network around Blue Crow and 885 Capital. Kit supplier is Joma.

The digital footprint is the most developed of its peer group for a La Liga 2-level club. Leganes reached two million total social media followers, with a Weibo presence indicating a deliberate Chinese market push. The club used Bundeling to build a centralised sponsor community platform. This digital infrastructure, built during the Blue Crow era, is the most commercially relevant asset and distinguishes Leganes from clubs with a physical fanbase but no digital engagement capability.

The 2023-24 Segunda title win was a genuine achievement for the Blue Crow model: a data-driven ownership group, without a marquee spending budget, delivering a first-ever division title. The one-season La Liga tenure and immediate relegation in 2024-25 reflects the difficulty of competing at the top level without a proportionate budget increase. La Liga relegation compresses broadcast revenue materially, which increases the commercial pressure to generate alternative income. If Blue Crow's ownership is confirmed as active, this is the context for a commercial approach: a management team that understands structured deals, has UAE-linked capital with digital market experience, and needs new revenue streams post-relegation.

6. Spezia Calcio

Serie B  /  Italy

Ownership

Spezia has had three separate owners in the space of four years, each of which is important context for understanding the current ownership profile. The sequence: the Platek family, led by Robert Platek, a partner at Michael Dell's MSD Capital, acquired Spezia in February 2021 for approximately EUR 25 million, just as the club achieved its first-ever Serie A promotion. After three seasons and failure to return from Serie B, the Plateks sold the entire share capital in February 2025 for EUR 1, a near-total write-down. FC32 Global Holdings lasted two months as owner before selling to RAM Spezia Holdings LP in April 2025.

Thomas S. Roberts is the current 100% owner through RAM Spezia. His background: Princeton economics (1985), Harvard Business School (Baker Scholar), 28 years at Summit Partners as CEO and Managing Partner of the $15 billion growth equity firm, then founder of Equality Asset Management in Boston. Roberts has injected over EUR 45 million as of December 2025, with a further EUR 25 million committed by May 2026. He has explicitly stated he will remain as owner even if the club drops to Serie C, a meaningful signal of long-term intent from an owner who has no compulsion to exit.

Roberts appointed Charlie Stillitano as club president. Stillitano is among the better-networked figures in American soccer administration: former General Manager of the NY/NJ MetroStars, co-founder of Relevent Sports Group (International Champions Cup), and currently President of TEG Sport North America. He is the operational commercial point of contact at the club. The combination of Roberts's capital commitment and Stillitano's commercial network is a materially different proposition from any of Spezia's three prior ownership regimes.

Commercial Activity

In September 2021, Spezia signed with Bitci, a Turkish blockchain firm, as a premium partner for three seasons, the first Serie A club to sign with Bitci. The deal ended five months later. By February 2022, Spezia and Sporting Lisbon both terminated after Bitci failed to make required payments. The club therefore has operational experience of a blockchain sponsorship deal and direct experience of what counterparty failure looks like from the inside. The distinction between an unregulated Turkish exchange and a MiCA-regulated European product is a specific due diligence point Roberts, with a private equity background, is equipped to evaluate.

Beyond the Bitci episode, the commercial programme is at an early stage. Kit supplier is Kappa across 2023-24 through 2025-26. No confirmed front-of-shirt sponsor post-Bitci is documented. The commercial build under Roberts and Stillitano is early-stage given the April 2025 acquisition. Stadio Alberto Picco holds approximately 10,336, small even by Serie B standards, which makes the club structurally dependent on non-matchday commercial revenue relative to clubs with larger stadium infrastructure. Instagram following is approximately 139,000, with an active English-language international social push signalling deliberate international brand development.

Three Serie A seasons, a relegation, a near-miss promotion run in 2024-25, and three consecutive ownership changes define a club that has experienced more structural turbulence than almost any comparable Italian side across this period. The Plateks' EUR 25 million entry and EUR 1 exit is a stark data point on how quickly football asset value can erode without promotion. Roberts's entry immediately post-Platek, at apparent book value, and his immediate capital injection of EUR 45 million plus a further EUR 25 million committed, indicates a genuine long-term bet rather than a financial arbitrage play. As of April 2026, Spezia are reported in the relegation zone of Serie B. Roberts has publicly committed to staying through Serie C if required.

Key note: The 2025-26 relegation risk is the primary near-term operational concern. A Serie C outcome would reduce broadcast revenues and fan engagement, creating additional financial pressure on an ownership group already absorbing significant capital. Roberts's stated commitment to remain regardless of league position is the mitigant. The commercial build under Stillitano is at an early stage; the Bitci episode means the club has operational familiarity with blockchain-based sponsorships and will not require category education in any commercial conversation.
