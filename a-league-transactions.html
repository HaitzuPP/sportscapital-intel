Structural risk pattern: All four dissolved clubs share a common profile — weak or absent private capital at inception, over-reliance on FFA support, and no viable buyer when financial distress hit. Western United is the most recent failure and the most instructive: the FIFA registration ban and federal court filing preceded the licence revocation by months, providing observable leading indicators.
Pylon Angle Silver Lake's AUD $140m entry at a ~$425m APL valuation (December 2021) is the league-level benchmark. The thesis was technology and media convergence: Silver Lake was simultaneously investing in City Football Group (Man City), and Melbourne City is a CFG club, creating an unusual dual-level exposure to the same league. Bill Foley's Auckland FC entry is the model for clean new-club origination in the A-League: existing multi-club infrastructure (Bournemouth, Moreirense), no inherited liabilities, expansion licence at lower cost than an acquisition. The Perth Glory distressed process (2023) demonstrates that receivership timelines in Australian sport are long, buyer processes are unreliable, and first-mover buyers face significant condition-precedent risk. Maverick's Newcastle takeover (2024) — following three years of APL interim control — is the other cautionary timeline: regulatory and league approval processes add 18-36 months of execution risk to any distressed acquisition.
